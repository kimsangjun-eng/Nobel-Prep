Strong, weak, or nonelectrolyte — how much of the solute actually becomes ions?

L-9
Electrolytes are substances that produce ions in water. How well a solution conducts electricity depends on how many ions are actually floating free in it: a strong electrolyte dissociates completely, a weak electrolyte dissociates only partially (and exists in dynamic equilibrium, ⇌, between ionized and un-ionized forms), and a nonelectrolyte dissolves as whole, neutral molecules that never ionize at all.
