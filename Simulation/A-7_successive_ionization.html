Successive Ionization

M(g) + energy → M+(g) + e- IE₁
M+(g) + energy → M2+(g) + e- IE₂
M2+(g) + energy → M3+(g) + e- IE₃
Radius: M > M+ > M2+ > M3+ — fewer electrons means less repulsion, so the same nuclear charge pulls the rest in tighter.
IE: IE₁ < IE₂ < IE₃ < … — each removal from an increasingly positive ion costs more energy than the last.
