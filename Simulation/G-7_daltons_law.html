Dalton's Law of Partial Pressures

Each gas contributes independently — total pressure is the sum of all partial pressures

G-7
Dalton's Law: in a mixture of gases, each gas exerts pressure independently as if it were alone. The total pressure equals the sum of partial pressures: Ptotal = PA + PB + … Each partial pressure is found by: Pi = χi · Ptotal, where χi = ni / ntotal is the mole fraction.
